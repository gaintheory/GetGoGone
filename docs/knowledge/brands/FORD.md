# Ford Brand Guide & Copy Model Knowledge

This brand guide outlines key selling points, typical model configurations (years 2010-2022), and compliant, persuasive copy angles for Ford inventory.

---

## 1. Brand Identity: The Hard-Working American Standard
Ford represents utility, toughness, and practical strength. For a BHPH buyer, a Ford vehicle (especially trucks like the F-150 or utility transit vans) is often a **revenue-generating tool**, not just transport. They need it to haul tools, tow equipment, or fit a large family. 

### Key Selling Points:
- **Built Ford Tough:** Celebrated work truck DNA with strong frames and cargo capacity.
- **Easy parts & Repairs:** Being the most common truck brand in America, parts are available at every local auto store and cheap to replace.
- **Engine Diversity:** Reliable 3.7L/3.5L V6 naturally aspirated, powerful EcoBoost, or classic 5.0L V8.

---

## 2. Core Models & Copy Angles

### Model A: Ford F-150 (Full-Size Truck)
*   **Typical Years:** 2010 - 2020
*   **Key Trims:** XL (basic work truck), XLT (chrome/power options), Lariat (leather/luxury)
*   **Key Specs:** 3.7L V6 (durable commuter truck), 3.5L/2.7L EcoBoost (tow torque), 5.0L V8 (traditional muscle)
*   **Copy Angles:**
    *   *The Workday Hero:* Focus on bed space, towing capacity, and durability for contractors, landscapers, or tradespeople.
    *   *The Roomy Family Cab:* Highlight the massive rear seat space in the SuperCrew configuration.
    *   *Example Hook:* *"Ready to get the job done? This F-150 XLT SuperCrew gives you a massive cab, a tough bed ready for cargo, and the towing power to pull your trailer."*

### Model B: Ford Edge / Explorer (SUVs)
*   **Typical Years:** 2011 - 2021
*   **Key Trims:** SE (standard), SEL (comfort tech), Limited / Platinum (premium)
*   **Key Specs:** 3.5L V6 (extremely durable naturally aspirated engine), 2.0T EcoBoost (fuel-efficient)
*   **Copy Angles:**
    *   *The Modern Family Explorer:* Focus on 3rd-row seating (Explorer), comfortable ride, high safety scores, and dual climate control.
    *   *Example Hook:* *"Pack up the family in style. This Explorer SEL offers comfortable 3rd-row seating, a smooth ride, and the reliability of Ford's proven V6 engine."*

### Model C: Ford Focus / Fusion (Sedans)
*   **Typical Years:** 2012 - 2020
*   **Key Trims:** S/SE (commuter value), SEL/Titanium (tech/luxury features)
*   **Key Specs:** 2.0L I4 (Focus), 2.5L I4 or 1.5T/2.0T EcoBoost (Fusion)
*   **Copy Angles:**
    *   *The Budget-Friendly Commuter:* Highlight high fuel economy (30+ MPG), sleek styling, and low insurance rates.
    *   *Example Hook:* *"Need a stylish commuter that sips gas? This Fusion SE offers a super smooth highway ride, modern styling, and excellent gas mileage."*

### Model D: Ford Transit / Transit Connect (Commercial/Cargo)
*   **Typical Years:** 2012 - 2020
*   **Key Specs:** 2.5L I4 (Transit Connect), 3.7L V6 or 3.5T EcoBoost (Transit Cargo)
*   **Copy Angles:**
    *   *The Business Builder:* Perfect for plumbers, electricians, mobile detailers, or delivery drivers. Emphasize low entry cost and massive cargo utility.
    *   *Example Hook:* *"Grow your business today. This Transit Connect offers massive cargo space, easy city parking, and excellent fuel economy to keep your business running."*
